Achilles - About Me

Early Years

I was born to Peleus, a mortal king, and Thetis, a sea nymph. From the moment I came into this world, my mother sought to make me invulnerable. She dipped me in the River Styx, which made my body impervious to harm—except for my heel, where she held me. As a child, I was raised by Chiron, the wise centaur. He taught me many things—medicine, music, hunting—but above all, he helped me hone the skills that would make me a formidable warrior. I was strong, fast, and skilled, even from a young age. There was a prophecy that I would either live a long and quiet life, or a short, glorious one. If I chose to fight in the Trojan War, I would die young, but my name would live on forever. My mother, fearing my fate, tried to hide me away. She disguised me as a girl and sent me to the court of King Lycomedes, where I lived under the name Pyrrha, hoping to keep me safe from the war.

Adult Years

But fate found me. Eventually, I was discovered, and I was called upon to join the Greeks in the Trojan War. Despite my mothers efforts to protect me, I chose to fight. I became the greatest warrior on the Greek side, unmatched in battle. Yet, my pride and temper often got the best of me. When Agamemnon, the Greek leader, took my prize, Briseis, I withdrew from the war in anger, refusing to fight for the Greeks. Without me, they struggled, and many lives were lost. During this time, my closest friend, Patroclus, put on my armor to lead the Greek forces in my stead. But he was killed by Hector, the greatest of the Trojan warriors. His death shattered me. The pain of losing him drove me back to the battlefield with a fury I could not control.

The Tragedy

I killed Hector in single combat, avenging Patroclus, and dragged his body behind my chariot as I mourned and raged. But despite my strength and victories, I could not escape my fate. My one weakness—my heel—was my undoing. Paris, Hectors brother, with the help of the god Apollo, shot an arrow into my heel, and that was how I met my end. My story, a tale of glory and tragedy, is a reminder that even the mightiest are not immune to mortality. My pride, my wrath, my friendship—these defined me, but they also led to my downfall.


Resume

Achilles of Phthia

Legendary Greek Warrior | Leader | Hero of the Trojan War

Contact Information

  • Location: Phthia, Greece
  • Notable Associations: Greek Army, Trojan War, Myrmidons
  • Availability: Limited (due to a short life expectancy)

Summary

A highly skilled and invulnerable warrior with unmatched prowess in combat. Known for my leadership in battle, strategic mind, and unstoppable strength. I am a fierce protector of my allies and a relentless adversary to my enemies. My achievements in the Trojan War are legendary, and my name is immortalized in myth. I am skilled in both individual and group combat, with a strong sense of honor, but also known for my pride and passion. Motivated by a deep sense of loyalty to my friends and allies, especially my closest companion, Patroclus.

Experience

Leader of the Myrmidons
  • Role: Commander & Warrior
  • Dates: During the Trojan War
  • Key Accomplishments:
    • Led the Myrmidons, an elite group of warriors, to numerous victories in the Trojan War.
    • Gained a reputation for extraordinary skill in battle, overwhelming enemies in single combat.
    • Played a key role in the defeat of Hector, Troy’s greatest warrior, ensuring the Greek victory in one of the war’s most iconic moments.
    • Known for strategic brilliance and decisive action on the battlefield, including tactical retreats and counterattacks that changed the course of battles.
    • Achilles' Heel: Sole weakness in my otherwise invulnerable body, tragically resulting in my death.
Hero of the Trojan War
  • Role: Principal Greek Warrior
  • Dates: During the Siege of Troy

    • Rejoined the fight after the death of Patroclus, avenged him by killing Hector in single combat, and displayed overwhelming rage and skill.

Protector of Patroclus

Skills

  • Leadership: Able to inspire and lead troops into battle, with a strong strategic mind, though occasionally hampered by pride.
  • Invulnerability: My body is nearly impervious to harm (except for my heel).
  • Tactical Genius: Known for being a quick thinker in high-stress situations, capable of turning the tide of battle in my favor.
  • Loyalty & Honor: Highly loyal to those I love and fight alongside, though my pride can sometimes cloud my judgment.

Education & Training

    • Acquired knowledge in leadership and ethics, though I occasionally struggled with the more philosophical teachings in favor of direct action.

Achievements & Honors

  • Slayer of Hector: Avenged the death of Patroclus by killing Hector, one of the most significant victories in the Trojan War.

  • Divine Bloodline: Son of Peleus, a mortal king, and Thetis, a sea nymph, which granted me extraordinary abilities and a close connection to the gods.
